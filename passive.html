    Passive income acquisition basics
    Seller's Discretionary Earnings — the total cash a business generates for one full-time owner-operator, including net profit + owner salary + non-cash add-backs. It's the raw earning power before debt service. Key caveat: seller-stated SDE is unverified until you review 2 years of tax returns and bank/processor statements. Over-stated SDE is the #1 fraud vector in small business sales.
    Seller financing means the seller takes back a note for part of the purchase price instead of requiring all cash at close. Benefits:
    • Lower down payment required
    • Seller has skin in the game — proves confidence in the numbers
    • Faster closing vs SBA (6–12 weeks vs 60–90 days)
    • Flexible terms (rate, amortization, standby period)
    We require it because our ~$200K capital must close the deal — full bank financing alone rarely works for this deal size.
    SBA 7(a) loans cover up to $5M with 10-year terms for business acquisitions. Key rules:
    • Minimum 10% equity injection (down payment)
    • Seller notes must be on full standby for 24 months to count as equity
    • Personal guarantee required
    • 3 years of tax returns from seller + 2 years of buyer financial statements
    SBA is best for larger, well-documented deals. For smaller deals (<$300K), seller financing alone is often faster and cheaper.
    • Lease risk — short remaining term (<5 years) with no renewal option is a dealbreaker
    • Machine age — top-load washers, 15+ year machines signal near-term capex
    • Coin-only — card reader absence limits revenue and attracts a declining demographic
    • Utility costs — gas/water are the biggest line items; verify with utility bills, not seller estimates
    • Competition — new laundromats opening nearby, or the property zoned for conversion
    • Seasonal / neighborhood — student-area mats can swing 30–40% seasonally
    Self-storage has some of the lowest operating costs of any real estate-adjacent business: no toilets, no kitchens, no daily tenant interaction. Key metrics to watch:
    • Occupancy ≥ 85% at underwriting (stabilized)
    • Cap rate vs local market — compare to recent trades
    • Climate control premium — CC units rent 20–30% higher
    • Competition map — supply pipeline within 3 miles
    • Management — on-site vs remote (software like Storagepug/Sitelink)
    Vending routes are only viable if you can service them economically. Our rule: SW Florida or same market as another PI/IP acquisition only — otherwise travel eats the margin. Key diligence items:
    • Location contracts — are they transferable? How long do they run?
    • Machine age and condition — older machines need cash investment
    • Route density — machines per square mile determines efficiency
    • CoC ≥ 20% is our minimum (margins are thin; management is real)
